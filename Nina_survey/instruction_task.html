In the following we will ask you to judge the climate friendliness of different carbon footprints.
This means we ask you to give your best educated guess about the size of the personal carbon footprint of a series of people with different lifestyles.
The presented persons will differ with respect to their diets, commuting and travel behavior as well as their electricity supply and recycling behavior.

A personal carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases (including CO2 and methane) that are emitted into the atmosphere as a result of an individual's consumption.
The carbon footprint thus includes emissions from driving, flying, using electricity, cooling or heating your home, purchasing goods and other sources.
It is measured in CO2 equivalents, so that other greenhouse gases additional to CO2 are included.
In other words, a personal carbon footprint reflects an individual's total impact on the climate system.

Please note that the individual carbon footprints you will assess are simplified, based on only 6 behaviors, so they don’t account for a person’s entire carbon footprint.
You will be asked to make this judgment on a scale from 1 (low carbon footprint) to 10 (large carbon footprint).
This means you do not have to estimate any numbers. Based on the six behaviors, a low carbon footprint (for a person in the United Kingdom) would equal about 1 ton of CO2 per year and a large carbon footprint about 5.5 tons of CO2.

Next you will see how the task will look like.
Then you will you will estimate the carbon footprints of 16 persons with different lifestyles.
Each time evaluating how small (=climate friendly) or large the carbon footprint seems to you.

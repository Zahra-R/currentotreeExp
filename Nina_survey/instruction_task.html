In the following we will ask you to judge the carbon intensity of different carbon foorprints.
This means giving your best educated guess about how much carbon emissions (CO2) you think the presented carbon footprint produces .
You will be asked to make this judgment on a scale from 1 to 10, so you do not have to estimate any numbers.

A personal carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases (including CO2 and methane) that are emitted into the atmosphere as a result of an individual's consumption,
their private investments, and their share of public services.
The carbon footprint thus includes emissions from driving, flying, using electricity, cooling or heating your home, purchasing goods and services, and public services (e.g., road construction, schools, and military).
In other words, a personal carbon footprint reflects an individual's total impact on the climate system.
